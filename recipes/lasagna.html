Lasagna

Description

Lasagna originated in Italy during the Middle Ages. The oldest transcribed text about lasagna appears in 1282 in the Memoriali Bolognesi ("Bolognesi Memorials"), in which lasagna was mentioned in a poem transcribed by a Bolognese notary; while the first recorded recipe was set down in the early 14th-century Liber de Coquina (The Book of Cookery).

Lasagna is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types, made of very wide, flat sheets. Lasagna can also refer to an Italian dish made of stacked layers of lasagna alternating with fillings such as ragù (ground meats and tomato sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses (which may include ricotta, mozzarella, and parmesan), and seasonings and spices.

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Onion and garlic
  • Tomato products
  • Sugar
  • Spices and seasonings
  • Lasagna noodles
  • Cheeses
  • Egg

Steps to prepare

  1. Make the meat sauce
  2. Cook the noodles
  3. Make the ricotta mixture
  4. Layer the lasagna
  5. Cover with foil and bake
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving
